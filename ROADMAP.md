# Habit-Tracker — Roadmap

Living list of planned features and deferred work. Newest ideas at the top of "Planned."

---

## ✅ Recently shipped
- **One-tap "Sync Apple Health" button.** A prominent button at the bottom of the app runs the **Copy My Stats** shortcut, then **auto-imports** the copied stats from the clipboard when you return to the app (best-effort — if iOS blocks the clipboard read without a tap, it falls back to opening the Apple Health sheet where Import is one tap). Turns the previous multi-step import into essentially one tap.
- **Day page: compact bottom bar.** The snacks summary and the "Riding an urge" button now share a single row at the bottom of the Day page (the urge button moved down from the top), reclaiming the vertical space of the old top banner so more of the day fits on one screen at the default text size.
- **Day page: auto food-logged + Vitamins on the Fat-Loss card.** Dropped the manual "Log Food — Cronometer" checklist row now that calories sync in automatically: the **Logged** lever auto-lights from the day's Cronometer calories in Apple Health (`dietKcal`), with the retired `cron` check kept as a fallback so old hand-ticked days still count. **Vitamins** moved to a tappable pill on the Fat-Loss card. Also hardened the Pages deploy workflow to retry the `configure-pages` build step (a transient failure there had silently stranded a merge).
- **Fat-Loss Day card + bonus incline treadmill (weight-loss Stage 2).** A compact card at the top of the Day page: a **weight-trend headline** (current bodyweight from Apple Health, a smoothed **lb/week rate** over ~8 weeks colored green when losing, and distance to the **175 lb goal**), the **trailing-week energy deficit** (from `nutritionSummary`), and a row of daily **fat-loss levers** — protein (vs 170 g), steps (vs 10k), incline, lift (lift days only), and food-logged — each lit from the day's real data. Plus a one-tap **bonus incline treadmill** logger: extra Zone-2 incline sessions (default 30 min @ 12%) stored on `state.inclBonus`, shown as undoable chips, lighting the incline lever and **folding their minutes into weekly Zone 2 volume** (surfaced in the Week-page Zone 2 caption). Day-page movement also gets **three daily walk checkboxes** (Morning · Midday · Evening — the ~70–80 min step base) instead of a single walk, and **Floor Mobility is now a plain done/not-done check** (the session counter is gone — one round is the routine) with its tight-areas chip and expandable stretch list kept.
- **Weight-loss training restructure: 3-day full-body split.** Replaced the 5-day upper/lower split with a **3-day full-body program** — **Full Body A / B / C on Mon / Wed / Fri**, with **Zone 2 cardio + mobility** on the off days (Tue/Thu) — the higher-retention choice during a fat-loss phase (each muscle trained 2-3×/week with more recovery, deficit does the fat loss). Rebuilt `WO_TEMPLATES` (dumbbell-only, balanced push/pull/squat/hinge/lunge/core, ~50-min sessions), the Day-page week (`S[]`), the Week meters (now **X / 3 workouts**, floor 2 / ceiling 3), the make-up sheet, and the AI-Coach prose + payload. Fiber target raised to **50 g**. Historical Tue/Thu sessions from the old split stay viewable (labeled *archived*); the full old split is preserved in `docs/5-day-split-archive.md`. First stage of the weight-loss focus.
- **Nutrition & energy-balance analysis (Cronometer → Apple Health).** Since Cronometer syncs to Apple Health, the existing Health import now also captures **protein, carbs, total fat, and fiber** (added to `HEALTH_META`/aliases and the import guide). A new Week-page **Nutrition & Energy Balance** card shows avg **calories in vs out** (out = Apple Health active + resting energy) and the resulting **daily deficit/surplus**, plus **protein vs 170 g** and **fiber vs 30 g** adherence and an avg-macros line — with an honest "Health's estimate, weigh the weekly trend" caveat. A `nutrition` block (deficit, macros, protein/fiber vs targets) flows into `coachPayload`, and a NUTRITION bullet asks the AI Coach to check the deficit fits the lose-fat-while-gaining-muscle goal against training load and bodyweight trend. First increment of the diet-analysis phase.
- **Snacks simplified — meal & timing concept removed.** Dropped the meal-anchored framing entirely: the 5 meal cards, the "After Each Meal" section, the post-meal coverage meter/dots, and the per-meal recommender / picker / "Ask coach" are all gone (~9.6k chars of meal engine removed). The Snacks tab is now a clean **day-long snack logger** — the weekly **Vigorous Bursts (VO₂max)** + **hard-minutes** meters on top, a **Today's Snacks** list (burst detail, hard-goal, insight, per-entry undo), and a **Log a Snack** catalog. All snack types kept; burst-detail logging, progression nudges, and the coach snack payload retained (minus meal-coverage fields).
- **Workout as a post-meal snack option.** Added **Workout** to the snack catalog (`sx_lift`, LIFT tag) so an afternoon lift can be logged as the movement for whatever meal it follows — one-tap, and the entry stamps the day's actual workout tag (e.g. “Workout · Upper Push”). Credits post-meal coverage like any other snack; the real session still lives in the Workout tab.
- **Exercise snacks: 5-meal schedule + walks unified + multiple movements per meal.** Reworked the Snacks model to match a real 5-eating-occasion day: **Breakfast** (treadmill burst) · **Mid-Morning** (muscle snack) · **Lunch** (walk) · **Dinner** (walk) · **Evening Snack** (muscle snack, not cardio — better before bed). **Walk is now a first-class snack** (one-tap, no sheet) so post-meal dog walks credit coverage, and **each meal can hold multiple movements** (e.g. Dinner = Walk + Squats). The recommender alternates squats/pushups across the two muscle slots and redirects off a just-trained group; the breakfast burst backs off to an easy option once a hard burst is already banked. Coverage meter and Week rollup now target 5/day. Option A fully done: the redundant Noon/Evening walk toggles are removed from the Day page (only the fasted morning Dog Walk remains there), so post-meal walks live in the meal cards.
- **Compact single-screen Day page.** Reorganized so the whole day fits without scrolling — including at the largest (A++) text size. Dropped the section time-eyebrows (MORNING / 8:30 WORKOUT / CARDIO / WALKS / STANDING) and merged rows into two label-less cards — a checklist (mobility, food, workout, cardio) and a movement grid (walks + standing). Removed redundant sub-times (walk times, standing-block windows), made walks single-line and standing blocks compact inline cells, slimmed the "Riding an urge" button, and tightened item padding + section spacing so it holds up at 20px base text.
- **Exercise-snack hard-minute goals.** A per-tempo-snack target of **3 min in Zone 3+ (2 counts as a floor)**: each logged cardio burst gets a graded pill — green "✓ target hit" at 3, accent "counts" at 2, muted "light" below. The weekly meter keeps **bursts as the primary lever** (frequency of the hard stimulus drives VO₂max, per the literature) with **hard minutes as a graded secondary metric** (target 18/wk = ~6 bursts × 3). Goals and targets flow into `coachPayload.exerciseSnacks` (`hardMinutesPerSnackTarget`, `hardMinutesWeeklyTarget`).
- **Exercise-snack burst metrics v2 — time-at-intensity.** The burst logger now captures what actually drives VO₂max, not just duration: **hard minutes (Zone 3+)**, **avg HR** and **max HR**, and **distance**, alongside time/speed/incline/RPE/format. Each logged burst gets a post-hoc **insight** — flags a mostly-warmup snack ("only ~50% was hard — shorten the lead-in") and classifies the peak against the age-based HR zones ("Zone 3, 79% max — a strong tempo; push one effort into Zone 4+ for VO₂max"). Weekly **hard-minutes** are tallied on the Snacks and Week pages (a Zone 3+ complement to the existing aerobic Zone 2 base, kept deliberately separate since vigorous work self-excludes from Zone 2), and hard-min / avg-HR / distance flow into `coachPayload.exerciseSnacks.burstDetails` for progression coaching.
- **Exercise-snack burst logging + coaching.** Cardio snacks (treadmill burst, incline walk, block run) now open a **burst detail form** instead of a plain minutes tap: a plain-language protocol ("short + genuinely hard, RPE 8-9; 3-4 × 30s sprints or a 2-3 min tempo; raise incline first, then speed"), a Sprints/Tempo toggle, and fields for duration, top speed, incline, RPE, and peak HR (Wahoo). Each burst is stored on the log entry's `det`, prefilled from your last one, and drives a **progression nudge** ("last: 6.0 mph · 8% · RPE 8 — try +1% incline") on the meal card. Burst details flow into `coachPayload.exerciseSnacks.burstDetails` so the AI Coach can prescribe the next step. Strength snacks keep the quick reps grid.
- **Exercise Snacks module (dedicated page).** A study-driven ["exercise snacks"](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC12354995/) tab: brief bouts anchored to breakfast/lunch/dinner. Each meal card suggests a context-aware exercise (deterministic recommender that reads today's lift muscles, cardio, and prior snacks; won't pile onto a just-trained group; steers cardio→mornings, squats→dinner) with a picker to swap and an optional **Ask coach** AI pick (reuses the Coach key, static fallback offline). Two headline meters: daily **post-meal coverage** (3/3) and a weekly **vigorous-burst (VO₂max)** tier (Floor 3 · Target 6 · Ceiling 9), mirroring the Zone 2 visual. Catalog tuned to a WFH lifestyle + home treadmill / outdoor route / bodyweight (treadmill burst, incline walk, around-the-block run, bodyweight squats, pushups, pull-ups, calf raises). Data lives in the per-day `state` blob (backup/weekly-read for free) and flows into `coachPayload.exerciseSnacks`. Day page keeps a compact summary that deep-links in; Week page gets a snack rollup.
- **Cardio Threshold Validation (Zone 2).** A `Zone 2 minutes` field on each cardio session (read off the Wahoo strap); 20+ min qualifies the session, and the Week page tracks weekly Zone 2 accumulation against locked tiers (Floor 90 / Target 150 / Ceiling 180+ min/wk) — the headline metric. High-intensity work (sprints/HIIT, Zone 3+) self-excludes and is never graded as a failed session. Per-session feedback is permission-framed (under-threshold = "still real expenditure, not a fail"); zone2Min flows into the AI coach payload. Built on the existing cardio logging rather than a parallel store. See `cardio-threshold-validation-spec-v2` (gdrive; supersedes v1).
- **Boredom-Binge Interrupt — urge-loop coach.** A dashboard "Riding an urge" button for the moment of bored/idle eating. One stateless AI call (reuses the Coach key/model) names the state, grants permission (soft 15-min timer, "still want it after? zero penalty"), and redirects restless energy into movement already owned (mobility, walk, air squats). Every tap logs an `urgeEvent` — outcome + the preceding activity (root-cause field) — to localStorage, with a ride-out rate "evidence mirror." Hard no-moralizing system prompt; static fallback so the button never dead-ends offline. See `boredom-binge-interrupt-spec-v1` (gdrive).
- **Feature B — dedicated Stretch & Mobility AI report.** A Workout/Mobility switch in the Coach tab generates a mobility report (Consistency · Coverage & Balance · Hold Durations · Match to Training · Recommendations) with add/drop/duration recommendations as approve-or-dismiss cards that update the active routine. Shared, kind-tagged report history with a filter. See `docs/feature-A-tightness.md` §A for the shared engine.
- **Feature A — chronic tight-area check-in → adaptive stretch/mobility.** Day-page tight-areas check-in (chronic list); the Stretch tab adapts with longer holds + targeted adds (approve-first, no reordering); optional AI "Tailor" pass. See `docs/feature-A-tightness.md`.
- Full **Aurora** redesign — light + dark theme with toggle, glass UI, floating tab bar.
- **AI Coach** approval workflow — approve/dismiss/undo recommendations (load, sets, reps, rest, add/remove exercises) for the workout templates.
- Apple Health **import** (22 metrics, paste + URL), reset/undo safety net, full codebase audit fixes + CI.
- 4 of 9 Aurora stretch illustrations (pigeon, forward fold, double pigeon, folded butterfly).
